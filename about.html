About

We built the OS
we wanted to use.

Fortis started as a frustration. Every mainstream Linux distribution ships compromises. Opt-out telemetry. Mutable roots. Services you didn't ask for. We decided to stop complaining and build something better.

Why Fortis exists

The threat landscape has changed. Sophisticated malware persists across reboots by writing to the filesystem. Supply chain attacks modify packages before they reach you. Remote services exfiltrate data without visible symptoms.

General-purpose Linux distributions weren't designed with these threats as first-order concerns. Fortis was. Every architectural decision starts from the question: what can an attacker do, and how do we make that harder?

The answer turned out to be immutability, verification, and isolation applied from the bottom up. Not as features you configure, but as constraints you build within.

What we believe

Opacity is a red flag

If you can't see what software is doing on your machine, you can't trust it. Fortis is fully auditable. Every component, every package, every build script is public.

Defaults are decisions

Most users never touch settings. That means defaults are the product. Ours default to private, secure, and minimal. Convenience is opt-in, not the other way around.

Community beats company

No single entity controls Fortis. Decisions happen in public. Governance is written down. Contributors from anywhere can propose changes, and the best argument wins.
